ðŸ”¹ What is a Lehenga?
A lehenga (also spelled as lengha or lehnga) is a three-piece outfit commonly worn by women in India and other South Asian countries. It's especially popular during weddings, festivals, and other grand occasions.The components of the Lehengaare the following:-

  • Lehenga (Skirt):- A long, embroidered, or embellished skirt that usually flares out from the waist. It can be straight-cut, mermaid style, A-line, circular, or paneled. Fabrics: Silk, georgette, velvet, net, satin, brocade, etc. Often features heavy work like zari, sequins, embroidery, mirror work, or prints.
  • Choli (Blouse/Top):- A fitted top that complements the lehenga. Styles vary: sleeveless, full sleeves, off-shoulder, backless, etc. Can be simple or heavily embroidered depending on the occasion.
  • Dupatta (Scarf/Stole):- A long, flowy piece of cloth draped over the shoulder or across the body. Adds grace and can be styled in many ways. Made from light fabrics like chiffon, net, organza, or silk.
